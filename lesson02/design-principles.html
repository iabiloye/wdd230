Virtual Hierachy

Meduim View

Visit Website

Visual hierarchy refers to the arrangement or presentation of elements on a page in a way that guides the viewer attention and emphasizes the importance of different components. It involves using design principles such as contrast, color, typography, and spacing to create a structured and organized layout. The goal is to lead users through content in a logical and intuitive manner, ensuring that key information stands out and the overall design communicates effectively. Visual hierarchy is crucial in web and graphic design to enhance user experience and convey information in a clear and impactful way.

Hick's Law

Ux Planet

Visit Website

Hick's Law underscores the idea that providing users with too many options can lead to increased decision-making time and potential cognitive strain. Designers can optimize user experiences by carefully curating choices and presenting information in a way that facilitates efficient decision-making.
